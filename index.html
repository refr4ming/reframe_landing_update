The toll on your body

Stress is not just in your head.

Chronic stress rewires your brain, weakens your immune system, and quietly shortens your life. The effects are measurable — and largely preventable.

Heart disease
Elevated cortisol raises blood pressure and increases cardiovascular risk by up to 40%.
Cognitive decline
Stress hormones shrink the prefrontal cortex, eroding focus, memory, and emotional control.
Sleep loss
60% of people with chronic anxiety report persistent insomnia, deepening both conditions.
Social withdrawal
Anxiety quietly dismantles relationships — severing the very connections that could offer relief.
